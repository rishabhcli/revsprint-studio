RevSprint Studio is a local-first commercial planning workspace built for early teams that need clarity faster than they need another dashboard.
In this walkthrough, I will show how messy founder notes become a live go-to-market sprint, ranked plays, and an exportable commercial brief.

Most early teams already have the signal they need.
What they do not have is one place where customer notes, objections, positioning gaps, and channel ideas turn into a believable operating plan.
That makes it easy for every week to feel reactive.

RevSprint Studio starts from that raw commercial input.
It turns scattered signals into structured opportunities, then scores them for impact, confidence, and effort so the team can see what deserves attention first.

From there, the workspace maps a six-week sprint.
The roadmap makes each week legible instead of chaotic.
It shows which plays should be tested, what message angles support them, and how the work should move from one owner to the next.

The execution board keeps that plan live as the sprint evolves.
Teams can move channel plays, review the active bets, and keep the commercial pressure visible without losing the structure of the plan.

RevSprint Studio also exports a concise commercial brief.
That brief is useful because the planning work does not stay trapped in the interface.
It can be shared in a founder review, an advisor sync, or the next operating meeting.

Under the hood, the architecture stays intentionally practical.
The app runs fully in the browser, keeps its state locally, supports an offline shell, and deploys as static files.
There is no backend dependency and no hidden demo setup.

That makes RevSprint Studio a strong fit for Dev Season of Code.
It is a focused product for sales and business development workflows, and it shows a clear before-and-after outcome in a format that is easy to review quickly.
